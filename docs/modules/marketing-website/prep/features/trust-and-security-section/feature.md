## Summary

A section (on homepage and/or dedicated page) that provides transparent information to build trust with students and parents. It covers security/privacy practices, how the system works, compliance standards (OWASP Top 10, data protection), and who built the platform. This section directly addresses parent and student concerns about data safety and platform legitimacy.

## User Story

As a parent considering whether to let my child use NicheLearner, I want to understand what data the platform collects, how it's protected, and how secure the system is, so I feel confident that my child's information is safe.

## Acceptance Criteria

- **Privacy policy is accessible** — link to full privacy policy is prominently placed (footer or dedicated section)
- **Data collection practices are clearly explained** — brief explanation of what data is collected during signup and use, and why
- **Data protection statement is provided** — clear statement about how student data is protected and stored
- **OWASP Top 10 compliance is mentioned** — brief statement that platform follows OWASP Top 10 security standards
- **Credibility information is provided** — information about who built NicheLearner and their background/mission
- **How the platform works is explained** — high-level explanation of how the adaptive quiz system works, what assessment means, how cross-topic references function
- **"No credit card required" is clearly stated** — emphasizes free trial truly is free with no credit card needed
- **Compliance with relevant regulations is documented** — if applicable, mention compliance with Indian student data protection laws
- **Trust signals are displayed** — certifications, badges, or statements that build confidence (if applicable)
- **Contact pathway for security concerns is provided** — users know how to report security issues or privacy concerns

## Priority

**High** — Trust and security information are critical for converting parent users (a key decision-maker). Without credibility markers, parents will be hesitant to allow trials.

## Dependencies

- `homepage` (security section should be on or linked from homepage)
