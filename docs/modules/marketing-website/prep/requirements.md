# Requirements: Marketing Website

This document defines the scope, users, and success criteria for the NicheLearner marketing website. Developers read only this file—no need to reference solution.md files.

## Product Context

### Problem Statement

Students preparing for the JEE Mains entrance exam in India lack personalized guidance that connects concepts across subjects (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics). Existing market competitors provide generic content without meaningful cross-referencing or realistic feedback on individual preparation levels. Many students cannot afford private tutoring to validate their solving methods or assess true readiness. This creates a critical gap: **students have no honest feedback on whether they are prepared and no clear roadmap of what to study next**.

### Core Value Proposition

NicheLearner solves this through three interconnected capabilities:

1. **Personalized proficiency-matched assessment** — quizzes automatically adapt to each student's demonstrated level, not one-size-fits-all
2. **Cross-topic reference navigation** — every topic maps to related concepts across subjects, showing how ideas interconnect
3. **Adaptive next-step recommendations** — each student receives concrete, ranked remediation paths based on actual performance, not guesswork

## Users & Personas

### Persona 1: JEE Aspirant (Student)

**Who they are:** High school student in India, typically post-10th grade, preparing for the JEE Mains entrance exam to gain admission to a top engineering college.

**What they want:**
- A realistic, honest assessment of their current preparation level relative to the exam
- Clear identification of which topics matter most and how concepts connect across Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics
- A personalized roadmap showing exactly what to study next, prioritized by their specific knowledge gaps

**What they fear:**
- Wasting months studying inefficiently or focusing on low-impact topics
- Realizing too late that they were using suboptimal solving methods (while shortcuts existed)
- Arriving at exam day unprepared with no warning signs, because no one gave them honest feedback

**How they use this module:**
- Discovers the platform via search, affiliate links, or college referrals
- Evaluates credibility markers on the marketing website (testimonials, product description, clear trial experience)
- Rapidly signs up for a free trial to test whether the adaptive quiz experience delivers on the promise of honest feedback
- Decides whether to convert to paid subscription based on trial results

---

### Persona 2: Parent

**Who they are:** Parent of a high school student preparing for JEE Mains in India, concerned with their child's educational outcomes and career prospects.

**What they want:**
- Confidence that their child is on track to succeed in the exam
- Transparency into their child's actual preparation level and progress
- Assurance that any money spent on tutoring, courses, or platforms actually improves outcomes
- Peace of mind that their child is not falling behind or wasting time on ineffective methods

**What they fear:**
- Their child failing the JEE Mains exam and missing the opportunity to attend a top engineering college
- Spending money on coaching or resources that don't improve outcomes
- Discovering too late that their child was struggling with fundamental concepts
- Feeling helpless because they cannot directly assess whether their child is learning effectively

**How they use this module:**
- Reviews the marketing website to evaluate product legitimacy and safety
- Seeks credibility markers (clear value proposition, transparent how it works, reviews)
- Influences or makes the decision to allow their child to start a free trial
- May monitor their child's progress and engagement to evaluate whether a paid subscription is worth the cost

---

### Persona 3: College Administrator / Educator

**Who they are:** Administrator, educator, or director of test prep at a college or coaching institution in India, responsible for helping their student body prepare for the JEE Mains exam.

**What they want:**
- To understand how NicheLearner works and whether it fills a gap in their institutional offerings
- Clear evidence that the platform provides measurable value (honest assessment, adaptive guidance) vs. generic competitors
- Information on how to deploy the platform at scale for their student population
- Understanding of institutional pricing, trial terms, and integration options

**What they fear:**
- Recommending a product to their students that doesn't deliver real value or is hard to use
- Missing an opportunity to offer differentiated test-prep guidance to their student body
- Unclear integration path or inflexible trial terms that don't work with their timeline

**How they use this module:**
- Discovers institutional trial opportunities through the marketing website
- Evaluates whether the core value proposition (cross-topic mapping, personalized assessment) differentiates the offering from competitors
- Understands how to offer free trials to their students and track engagement/outcomes
- May engage with institutional pricing or partnership options for bulk deployments

## Module Objective

### What This Module Does

The marketing website is the **primary discovery and entry point** for NicheLearner. It serves three critical functions:

1. **Establishes credibility and product clarity** — communicates the core value proposition (personalized assessment, cross-topic references, adaptive guidance) to students and parents who have no prior exposure
2. **Enables individual signup for free trials** — removes friction so students and parents can test the product with minimal commitment
3. **Supports institutional acquisition** — enables college partnerships by providing a clear reference point where college administrators can evaluate and deploy the product at scale for their students

### Why This Module Matters

Without a credible, accessible marketing website, the core differentiation (cross-topic reference mapping and proficiency-matched assessments) remains invisible to target users. The primary acquisition strategy—college partnerships with free-trial offers—depends entirely on having a high-trust landing page where:

- College administrators can refer their student bodies via links or partnerships
- Individual students and parents can rapidly form trust and begin a trial without friction
- Product value is communicated clearly and credibly

The marketing website is a **prerequisite for executing the college outreach strategy** and proving the core conversion hypothesis (5% of trial users → paid subscribers).

### Scope: One Sentence

A high-trust marketing and trial-entry website that clearly communicates NicheLearner's value proposition, supports frictionless signup for students and parents, and enables institutional partnerships with colleges.

## Must-Have Features

**These capabilities are required for launch. Without them, the module does not solve its core problem.**

### For All Visitors
- **Clear, credible value proposition** — website prominently communicates the three core capabilities (personalized assessment, cross-topic references, adaptive guidance) in language students and parents understand
- **Product overview without detailed content** — explains WHAT the platform does and WHY it matters, without listing specific course subjects, topics, or detailed learning content (that belongs in the platform)
- **Credibility markers** — transparent information about the platform (how it works, what data it uses, security/privacy stance) to build trust with students and parents
- **Free-trial signup flow** — simple, frictionless form to begin a trial account (minimal friction = quick conversion to testing the product)

### For Students & Parents
- **Persona-specific messaging** — the website addresses both student concerns (honest feedback, clear next steps) and parent concerns (credibility, outcomes, investment justification)
- **Call-to-action to free trial** — prominent, easy-to-find signup button with clear messaging ("Try free for 1 month" or equivalent)
- **Mobile-responsive design** — website works seamlessly on phones and tablets (students and parents browse on mobile)

### For College Administrators & Partners
- **Institutional trial information** — clear explanation of how colleges can offer the platform to their students (partnership model, trial terms, referral link, contact info)
- **College-specific value proposition** — addresses college concerns (institutional scale, bulk deployment, measurable student outcomes, integration ease)
- **Contact/partnership pathway** — easy way for college administrators to inquire about partnerships or bulk deployments

### Security & Compliance
- **OWASP Top 10 compliance** — website architecture and authentication follow OWASP Top 10 security standards (required for handling student sign-ups and building trust)
- **Trial access tracking** — backend system to track which signups came from which source (organic, affiliate, specific college), essential for measuring conversion and ROI
- **Trial revocability** — ability to revoke/expire trial access after the free period ends or upon subscription decision (required for the trial-to-paid conversion model)

## Optional Features

**Nice-to-haves that may be added in future iterations if resources allow, but are not blockers for launch:**

- **Testimonials or success stories** — real quotes from students or parents about how the platform helped them (builds credibility, especially from students who've used it in beta)
- **Comparison with competitors** — side-by-side explanation of how NicheLearner differs from other JEE prep platforms (if competitors are named and documented; currently not in scope)
- **Detailed explainer videos** — short video showing the quiz experience or guidance workflow (helps users understand product faster, but landing-page text + signup CTA may be sufficient to drive trials)
- **FAQ or knowledge base** — common questions about free trials, subscriptions, privacy, security (can be phased in post-launch)
- **Social proof badges** — college logos, download count, user count (only if these exist and are notable; if not, omit)
- **Affiliate or referral program** — structured way for existing users or marketing partners to refer new users (business decision, not a website feature)
- **Blog or content marketing** — educational content about JEE prep strategies, study tips, etc. (explicitly NON-GOAL per module definition, but could be considered for long-term SEO)

## Out of Scope

**These capabilities are explicitly NOT part of this module. They belong in the product platform or other initiatives.**

- **Detailed learning content** — does not list or teach course subjects, topics, or learning materials (e.g., no "Physics Topics" page listing all chapters; that's in the product)
- **Bulk-email or WhatsApp marketing campaigns** — website focuses on web discovery (SEO, affiliate links, direct referrals); does NOT execute outbound bulk messaging
- **Content marketing engine** — does not serve as a blog, tutorial site, or strategy guide with long-form educational content
- **Substitute for the main product** — website is an entry point only; the adaptive quiz experience, personalized assessment, and cross-topic guidance happen in the product platform, not on the website
- **Payment processing or subscription management** — website collects trial signup; subscription billing and account management happen in the product platform
- **Direct tutor/coach services** — no live chat, tutoring, or 1-on-1 coaching through the website

## Key Constraints

**Hard requirements and limitations that shape the solution:**

### Timeline & Deployment
- **College partnership pilots require timely deployment** — the website must be live and discoverable before or during institutional outreach (solution-context.md notes "near-term" college pilots, but no hard launch deadline is documented; confirm timeline with product team)
- **Revocable trial access essential for metrics** — the website cannot launch without backend support for tracking trial expiration and revocation, since trial-to-paid conversion measurement depends on it

### Security & Compliance
- **OWASP Top 10 compliance** — mandatory security framework for handling student data, authentication, and signup information (solution-context.md)
- **Student data privacy** — any personally identifiable information (email, name, age) collected during signup must comply with privacy and educational data protection standards applicable in India [**specific regulatory requirements to be confirmed by product team**]
- **Payment/billing compliance** — if trial expiration or subscription payment happens on the website, it must meet Indian payment processing and financial compliance standards [**specific requirements to be confirmed by product team**]

### User Experience
- **Minimal friction signup** — free-trial signup form should take <2 minutes to complete; college referral links must work without requiring signup on the website (direct link to platform trial signup)
- **Mobile-first design** — students browse and sign up primarily on mobile; website must be responsive and optimized for mobile experience

### Integration & Tracking
- **Trial access tracking** — website must integrate with the product platform's user authentication and trial management system to:
  - Tag signups by source (organic, affiliate, specific college referral)
  - Enforce trial duration and expiration
  - Track signup-to-trial-start conversion
  - Report trial-to-paid conversion for success metrics
- **Affiliate or college referral attribution** — ability to track which signups came from specific college partnerships or affiliate links (required to measure institutional channel ROI)

### Technical Stack
- **Frontend:** Vue.js (per solution-context.md)
- **Backend:** Golang (per solution-context.md) for serving website and managing trial signup flow
- **Database:** MySQL (per solution-context.md) for storing signup and trial tracking data
- **Infrastructure:** AWS (per solution-context.md) for hosting, scaling
- **API Architecture:** REST API (per solution-context.md) for integrating website signup with product platform's authentication and trial management
